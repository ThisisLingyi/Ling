JTBD in Data Management

Building the platform’s first shared, evidence-backed map of what users are really trying to get done when they get their data in.

Lingyi Ge · Foundational research lead
One-year roadmap · 5 min read
[ hero — GDI job map artifact ]

The problem

Two data domains, one platform — and no shared understanding of what users were actually trying to accomplish. Product and design each carried their own assumptions, and roadmaps were argued from opinion rather than evidence.

There was no foundational research to point to. So I built one — a Jobs-to-be-Done library from scratch, on a one-year roadmap.

The approach

A mixed-method program that triangulated qualitative depth with behavioral signal, synthesized into artifacts the whole org could use.

01 · Discover

In-depth interviews & contextual inquiry across both data domains.

02 · Synthesize

Thematic & affinity analysis into a structured job map.

03 · Activate

Personas, journey maps & a living library the teams reference.

What the job map revealed

Mapping the core job — get the right data in, to the right place, with confidence — surfaced the stages where users stall and where the platform could remove friction.
